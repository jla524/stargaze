- **[Why we should train AI in space](https://lumenorbit.github.io/wp.pdf)**  
  Training AI on Earth requires massive energy and cooling resources, leading to high CO2 emissions and water usage. In space, sun-synchronous orbits provide continuous solar power at 1366 W/m². The vacuum enables efficient radiative cooling to near-absolute zero, achieving PUE as low as 1.05. This eliminates water and CO2 costs while enabling hyperscale clusters. Radiation effects are manageable with error-correcting memory and radiation-hardened hardware.

- **[Towards a future space-based, highly scalable AI infrastructure system design](https://research.google/blog/exploring-a-space-based-scalable-ai-infrastructure-system-design/)**  
  The Sun emits 3.86×10²⁶ W, far exceeding Earth's capacity. Project Suncatcher proposes TPU-equipped satellites in low Earth orbit, interconnected by terabit/s free-space optical links. Close formations (kilometer-scale) enable high-bandwidth communication. Trillium TPUs withstand 2 krad radiation doses, exceeding mission requirements. Orbital dynamics models confirm stable configurations with minimal propulsion. Projected launch costs under $200/kg make energy-competitive data centers feasible by 2030s.

- **[ASCEND feasibility study](https://ascend-horizon.eu)**  
  ASCEND demonstrates space data centers for Europe's net-zero data sovereignty. Solar illumination and space vacuum provide low-PUE operation. On-orbit processing reduces latency for space data and minimizes downlink volume. Modular hyper-structures, assembled robotically, scale to thousands of tons via hundreds of annual launches. Lifecycle analysis shows environmental benefits with low-carbon launchers, targeting operational systems by 2030.
